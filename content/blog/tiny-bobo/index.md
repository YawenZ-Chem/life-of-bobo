I flew to New York over Thanksgiving to pick up Bobo and bring him home to Boston. When the breeder opened the trunk of the car, Bobo started yelling at the top of his tiny lungs into the freezing, snowy wind. I was stunned. How could such a tiny cat make that much noise?

He yelled all the way from the hotel to the airport, then kept going until we reached home in Boston. The breeder must have sent me home with the chattiest kitten in the litter. Still, I figured that any kitten capable of making that much noise had to be healthy. So perhaps it wasn’t such a bad thing.

Once we arrived at the apartment, he ran from room to room, meowing the entire time. Eventually, I realized that he was looking for the litter box. I pointed him toward it with my finger, and he jumped right in. From that moment on, he always knew where to find it. Apparently, he was pretty clever too.

At first, Bobo was barely the size of two palms. The breeder had made him a little outfit from an old sock, and when we got home, I cut another one for him myself.

The truth was that Bobo didn’t particularly enjoy wearing clothes. Even the best-fitting outfit seemed to restrict his movement, so most of the time, I was perfectly happy to let him run around the apartment naked.

What amazed me was that from the moment he arrived, he followed me everywhere. He didn’t seem to need any time to get used to me, study me, or decide whether I could be trusted.

When I sat at my desk, he would immediately climb into my lap. He would sit upright for a little while, tuck himself into a tiny loaf, and eventually fall fast asleep.

He would sit upright for a little while, tuck himself into a tiny loaf, and eventually fall fast asleep.

Bobo was so small then that, even when he sat up as tall as he could, his head could barely clear the edge of the desk. Every day while I worked at my computer, he would tilt his head back and quietly watch me.

Sometimes I couldn’t tell whether he simply wanted to be near me or whether he genuinely wanted to participate in the work. If only he could have written my lab reports for me.

At first, I was nervous about leaving Bobo to sleep alone in the living room. In theory, an independent little bald cat should have had a sleeping place of his own.

But he was so tiny, and the apartment wasn’t very warm, so on his very first night, I brought him into my bed.

{{< /story-row >}}

From then on, he simply assumed that my bed was where he belonged. I still don’t know whether he was clever, stubborn, or knowingly taking advantage of a loophole.

Every night, as soon as I turned off the lights and walked into the bedroom, he would trot happily after me.

{{< /story-row >}}

I had actually bought him a cat bed that I thought was warm and adorable. I deliberately placed it in the bedroom so that we could be roommates who slept in the same room. Bobo, however, continued to insist on sleeping in my bed.

Eventually, I moved the cat bed beside my desk. He was willing to curl up inside it while I worked, as long as he could keep an eye on me.

Sometimes he would wake up and forget that his tongue was still sticking out.

{{< /story-row >}}

When I lay on the sofa scrolling through my phone, Bobo would sometimes collapse beside me in a very peculiar position.

Then, just as I became completely absorbed in my phone, he would grow jealous, rush over, pull my hand away from it, and forcefully shove his little bald head into my palm.

His determination to be petted always astonished me. How could such a tiny body push with so much force?

{{< /story-pair >}}

Very quickly, I became accustomed to Bobo’s constant companionship. Sometimes I had to hold him while I worked, leaving me with only one hand for typing.

The way I held Bobo never really felt like holding a cat. He would rest both front paws against my chest and sometimes even wrap them around my neck, as if I were carrying a small child.

{{< /story-row >}}

Other times, I had to arrange my legs into a very particular shape so that he could sleep securely between them.

While I worked, I would occasionally poke the sleeping Bobo. He felt like a warm peach.

Back then, I was so jealous of his superpower: he could fall asleep anywhere, at any moment, and then wake up again completely refreshed and full of energy.
